# Ledgr Pitch: Talking Points & Responses

**Prepared for:** Investor meetings, pitch events, founder interviews
**Updated:** May 31, 2026

---

## Opening Statement (90 Seconds)

**Hook:** "There's a regulatory time bomb ticking in the UAE. On January 1st next year, the FTA e-invoicing mandate forces 600,000 SMEs to completely overhaul their accounting workflows. Most still use spreadsheets. Many will face fines."

**Problem:** "Existing solutions—Xero, QuickBooks, Zoho—don't understand UAE compliance. They were built for the US market. Implementing their solutions costs businesses AED 30K+ in consulting just to get compliant."

**Solution:** "Ledgr is built from the ground up for UAE. We automate FTA e-invoicing, VAT compliance, corporate tax, and payroll. An SME goes from 80 hours a month of compliance work to 2 hours. No consulting needed."

**Opportunity:** "The UAE SME market spends $180M+ annually on accounting software. We're solving an urgent, mandatory problem. And we're 18 months ahead of competitors who are just now realizing FTA is real."

**Ask:** "We're raising $500K-1M to accelerate customer acquisition and build the mobile app. We project $600K ARR by month 12, profitability by month 24."

---

## Key Talking Points

### Problem & Opportunity

**Q: Why is this timing so critical?**
A: "The FTA mandate becomes law January 1, 2027—hardcoded into legislation. Penalties start day one. That's 7 months away. Most SMEs have no idea what e-invoicing is. When reality hits in December, demand will spike. We want to own that wave."

**Q: How big is the actual market?**
A: "UAE has 600,000 registered SMEs. 85% use legacy or manual accounting. The mandatory compliance market is 500,000+ businesses. If we capture 3% by Year 3, that's 15,000 customers at AED 600-1,300/month blended ARPU. That's AED 100M+ ARR."

**Q: What about Wafeq (the local competitor)?**
A: "Wafeq has 2,000 customers but high churn. Their tech is outdated (built 2010-era). They don't have the AI/automation layer we built. They're scrambling to add FTA compliance now. We're shipping it day one, January 2027."

---

### Product Differentiation

**Q: How is Ledgr different from Xero/QuickBooks?**
A: "Three things:
1. **Native FTA compliance** – Xero/QB require 3-6 months of consulting to set up. We're plug-and-play.
2. **AI automation** – We automate invoice OCR, bank reconciliation, tax calculations. Competitors don't.
3. **Local expertise** – Arabic UI, MOHRE payroll integration, UAE tax knowledge baked in. Competitors are US-centric."

**Q: What's your competitive moat?**
A: "18-24 month head start in FTA automation. By the time competitors build this, we'll have 10,000+ customers, data network effects, and brand dominance. Plus, the FTA learning curve is steep—we've already solved it."

---

### Technology & Scalability

**Q: What's your tech stack? Can it scale?**
A: "Frontend: Static HTML/CSS/JS (serverless, CDN-delivered). Backend: TypeScript on AWS Lambda + RDS. This architecture scales to 1M+ users with 10x current infrastructure. Cost per customer actually *decreases* at scale."

**Q: How do you handle data security?**
A: "All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). We're hosted in AWS Dubai (me-south-1), meeting FTA data residency requirements. We have ISO 27001 certification and complete audit trails."

**Q: What about integrations?**
A: "We have APIs for QuickBooks, Xero, Plaid (banks), Stripe, and WhatsApp. We're adding Sage and Microsoft Dynamics in Q3. We're platform-agnostic—your customers already use these tools."

---

### Business Model & Unit Economics

**Q: How do you make money?**
A: "Freemium SaaS. Essential (AED 199/month) for micro businesses, Pro (AED 599) for small, Business (AED 1,299) for growth, Enterprise (custom) for 50+ employees. Blended ARPU: AED 600 (USD 160/month)."

**Q: What's your CAC and LTV?**
A: "CAC: USD 8-50 via partnerships and organic. LTV: USD 1,200-5,850 depending on tier. Payback period: 12-15 months Year 1, dropping to 6-8 months by Year 3."

**Q: What's your retention/churn?**
A: "We target 95%+ MRR retention and 3-5% monthly churn. With network effects (accountant referrals, SME word-of-mouth), we expect 115% NRR (expansion revenue from upsells)."

**Q: Path to profitability?**
A: "We break even in month 24. Gross margins are 75% Year 1, 80%+ by Year 3. With discipline, we reach profitability while still growing 30%+ YoY."

---

### Go-to-Market

**Q: How will you acquire customers?**
A: "Dual funnel:
1. **B2B2C (accounting firms)** – Partner with 300+ local accounting firms. They become resellers. Highest CAC efficiency.
2. **Direct SME** – Google Ads, LinkedIn, content marketing. Higher CAC but direct relationship.
3. **Government partnerships** – Dubai Chamber, DIFC FinTech, economic free zones (low-cost channels)."

**Q: What's your first 90 days?**
A: "Close 3 accounting firm partnerships (500+ SMEs). Hit 1,000 customers via organic. Launch mobile app (iOS/Android). Achieve #1 SEO ranking for 10+ UAE fintech keywords."

**Q: How will you spend the fundraise?**
A: "40% engineering (mobile, advanced AI). 25% sales/partnerships. 20% marketing/SEO. 10% regulatory/legal. 5% ops/finance."

### Investor Perspective

**Q: Why should I invest in Ledgr vs. other FinTech?**
A: "Timing + market reality.
- **Urgent mandate:** FTA enforcement is law, not optional. Demand is inelastic.
- **Large TAM:** $180M+ UAE market + expansion to GCC.
- **Capital efficient:** Static frontend scales to millions on minimal infrastructure. We're not burning cash on servers.
- **Experienced founder:** [Your background/exits].
- **18-month head start:** Competitors haven't built this yet."

**Q: What's your exit strategy?**
A: "Multiple paths:
1. **Strategic acquisition** – Xero, QuickBooks, Zoho may buy us for UAE market access.
2. **Regional expansion** – Turn UAE success into GCC play (Saudi, Qatar, Oman). AUM expands 3-4x.
3. **IPO** – If we hit $50M+ ARR, could pursue regional IPO (DFM, ADX, or NASDAQ).
Series B and C would fund geographic expansion and product diversification (payroll solutions, supply chain finance)."

**Q: What are your biggest risks?**
A: "Three main risks:
1. **Regulatory change** – FTA could delay or modify the mandate (unlikely but possible). Mitigation: We're in constant dialogue with FTA; we can pivot quickly.
2. **Competitor execution** – Xero/QB could launch native UAE compliance (possible). Mitigation: We move fast; our 18-month head start buys time to become entrenched.
3. **Market penetration** – SMEs might ignore the mandate initially (possible). Mitigation: Fines and penalties force adoption by mid-2027. We're betting on enforcement."

### Objection Handling

**Objection: "Why not just use Xero/QuickBooks?"**
Response: "They work for global accounting. But they're not designed for UAE. You need customization, local support, and FTA integration. That costs more than our all-in-one solution. Plus, you're waiting for them to build FTA features. We're shipping with it."

**Objection: "Wafeq is already here."**
Response: "Wafeq has 2,000 customers but high churn. They don't have the product innovation we do. We're building for the future; they're maintaining legacy tech. By the time they catch up, we'll have network effects they can't compete with."

**Objection: "Why should I trust a pre-revenue startup with my financials?"**
Response: "Fair concern. Three answers:
1. We're ISO 27001 certified with UAE data residency.
2. We integrate with QuickBooks/Xero, so your data isn't siloed.
3. We have professional indemnity insurance and regulatory backing (in discussions with FTA and DIFC).
Most importantly, the alternative—spreadsheets or outdated software—is riskier."

**Objection: "What happens if you run out of money?"**
Response: "We're focused on unit economics and profitability. With positive CAC payback (12-15 months) and 75%+ gross margins, we can self-fund after Series A if needed. Plus, strategic partners (QuickBooks, Xero) could accelerate acquisition if we wanted. We have options."

**Objection: "Your SAM is only UAE. How do you scale?"**
Response: "Year 1-2: Focus on UAE TAM (500K SMEs, $180M market). Year 2-3: Expand to GCC (Saudi, Qatar, Oman = 3-4x the market). By Year 4, we have localized versions for MENA region. Our FTA playbook becomes our Saudi/Qatar playbook. The moat we build scales regionally."
